The Weight of the Virtual World

While the internet feels like "air," it lives within a massive, physical architecture. To understand our impact, we must look at the hidden heart of our digital lives:1

  • Energy Hunger: These vast warehouses of servers are constantly running, currently consuming about 1-1.5% of all electricity on Earth.1
  • Constant Thirst: To stop machines from overheating, data centers require gallons of water every day for cooling.2
  • Physical Roots: Every cloud storage folder actually sits on a piece of hardware that requires rare minerals mined from the soil.3
  • Natural Solutions: Many new facilities are being built in colder climates to use the Earth’s natural wind for cooling instead of electricity.2
